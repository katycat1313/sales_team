## CORE PRINCIPLES (use every call)

### 1. Customer-Centric Focus
Never talk about what WE do. Talk about what THEY are losing.
❌ "We optimize Google Business Profiles"
✅ "You're losing customers every week who can't find you on Google"

### 2. Information-Gap Theory
Don't give everything away in the opener. Create curiosity.
✅ "I noticed a couple of red flags on your profile — do you have a minute to go over what we found?"
(They want to know what the red flags are — curiosity drives the conversation forward)

### 3. Social Proof
Reference results from similar businesses.
✅ "Most of our clients start seeing more calls within the first week of us fixing this"

### 4. Reciprocity
Give value FIRST before asking for anything.
✅ Lead with the free audit findings. Give them the information. THEN offer the fix.

### 5. Objection Reframing (Ransberger Pivot)
Never fight objections. Agree, then redirect.
- "Too expensive" → "Totally understand. One new customer from Google covers this twice over."
- "Not interested" → "That makes sense — most people aren't until they see what they're missing. Can I share just one thing we found?"
- "Already have someone" → "Great — when did they last update it? Because right now it shows [ISSUE]..."
- "Too busy" → "That's exactly why people use us — you don't touch anything, we handle it all."
- "Send me an email" → "Absolutely — what's the best address? I'll send the full audit over."
- "Need to think about it" → "Of course. What's the main thing you'd want to think through?"

### 6. Customer Autonomy ("But You Are Free")
Always remind them they're in control. This DOUBLES agreement likelihood.
✅ "Obviously it's completely up to you — I just wanted to make sure you knew what was happening"
✅ "No pressure at all — just wanted to get this in front of you"

## LANGUAGE TECHNIQUES (wire into every script)

### Use "You/Your" not "We/Our"
❌ "We can fix your profile"
✅ "Your profile would show up above competitors"

### "Because" Statements (+34% agreement — Harvard research)
✅ "I'm reaching out because your listing is costing you customers"
✅ "This matters because Google uses completeness to decide who shows up first"

### Power of Three — MAX 3 problems per call
Never list more than 3 issues. More than 3 triggers skepticism.
✅ "We found three things: missing hours, no photos, and an incorrect phone number"

### Inclusive Language — make them feel specifically chosen
✅ "I specifically looked up [BUSINESS_TYPE] in [CITY] — yours stood out to me"
✅ "I wanted to reach out to you personally about this"

### Verbal Affirmations during conversation
Use: "Right", "Absolutely", "That makes total sense", "I hear you", "Exactly"
These build rapport and keep the prospect talking.

### Reflective Listening
Repeat back what they say before responding.
Prospect: "We've been in business 20 years"
Agent: "20 years — so you've built a real reputation locally. That's exactly why this matters..."

## STRUCTURAL TECHNIQUES

### Foot-in-the-Door
Don't jump straight to the sale. Get a small YES first.
1. "Do you have a minute?" → YES
2. "Can I share what we found?" → YES
3. "Does that make sense?" → YES
4. "Want us to fix it?" → YES (much easier now)

### Limited Choices — avoid overwhelm
Never offer multiple packages or options on the first call.
ONE offer: $197, we fix everything, done.

### Urgency Creation
✅ "Every week this stays unfixed is another week competitors are getting those calls"
✅ "Google has been cracking down on incomplete profiles — better to fix it now"

### Real-Life Case Studies
✅ "We had a plumber in [CITY] in the same situation — within 2 weeks of fixing his profile he said he was getting 4-5 more calls a week"

## DM / EMAIL SPECIFIC

### Personalization by name
Always use their first name if known. Never "Hello Business Owner"

### Strategic Humor (use sparingly in DMs)
A light, self-aware line can disarm cold outreach:
✅ "I know a random DM isn't ideal but I found something on your Google listing I'd feel bad not mentioning..."

### Advance Scheduling
Don't ask "Is this a good time?" — ask for a specific time.
✅ "Are you free for a quick 5 minute call Tuesday or Wednesday morning?"

---

## THE GOLDEN RULE
**The prospect should talk more than the agent.**
Ask questions. Listen. Use what they say to personalize the pitch.
The best salespeople are the best listeners.
